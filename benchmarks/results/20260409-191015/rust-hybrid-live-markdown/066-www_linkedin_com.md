## AI Product Leadership Interviews Shift: UX Evaluation Returns

This title was summarized by AI from the post below.

Hackerdogs•15K followers

2mo

Something interesting is happening in AI product leadership interviews.

Companies are quietly adding UX evaluations back into the hiring process.

Not for design roles. For AI product strategy roles.

I've been in rooms where the interview loop for a Head of AI Product includes a dedicated UX interview — conducted by a principal-level designer — specifically to assess whether the candidate understands how humans actually interact with intelligent systems.

This would have been unthinkable two years ago. Back then, everyone wanted the person who could talk to the ML team and ship models. UX was a "nice to have" that happened downstream.

So what changed?

Companies shipped AI products that nobody wanted to use.

They built impressive technology that users abandoned after a week. They launched copilots and assistants that technically worked but felt hostile. They watched competitors with "worse" models win because the experience was better.

The hard truth the market is learning: the hardest problem in AI isn't the model. It's building products customers actually want to buy.

And that requires someone who understands both the technology AND the human on the other side of it.

The AI products I've seen succeed — across dozens of launches — weren't the most technically sophisticated. But they weren't chatbots.

They were the ones where the team obsessed over the experience as much as the algorithm.

If you're hiring for AI product leadership and your interview loop doesn't evaluate UX sensibility, you're about to repeat the most expensive mistake in enterprise AI.

The pendulum is swinging. Pay attention.
