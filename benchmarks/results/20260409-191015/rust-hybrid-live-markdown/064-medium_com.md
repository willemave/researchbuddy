Nearly 85% of all data science and AI projects never make it into production. Think about that. The vast majority of time, talent, and money poured into machine learning innovation ends in a dead end. It’s not a failure of algorithms; it’s a failure of leadership.

This isn’t just another tech challenge. It’s a crisis of ML project management. Traditional software development plans crumble under the weight of ML’s experimental workflows and data dependencies. Without a new playbook, teams are destined to build brilliant models that solve no one’s problems. But a small fraction of projects succeed, and they all have something in common: a rigorous, human-centric approach to management.

**Start with Why: The Power of ML Goal Setting**

The single biggest pitfall in machine learning is building a “solution in search of a problem.” Teams get lost chasing algorithmic perfection, forgetting the business goal. This is where Objectives and Key Results (OKRs), a framework pioneered at Intel and perfected at Google, becomes your most powerful tool for effective ML project management.

An Objective is your qualitative, ambitious goal. Key Results are the specific, measurable outcomes that prove you’ve achieved it.
