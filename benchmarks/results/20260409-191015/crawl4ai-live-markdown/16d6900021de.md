#  AI Won't Save You, Reinvention Will: Gopi Rangan Podcast

I was recently a guest on [Gopi Rangan](https://www.linkedin.com/in/gopirangan?trk=public_post-text)’s Sure Shot Entrepreneur podcast, in a conversation with longtime friend and fellow traveller, [Bhaskar Ghosh](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ghoshbhaskar?trk=public_post-text). We called the podcast “AI will not save you, Reinvention Will!” (though BG renamed it for film-buffs in his post!). We touched on many different topics from our shared journeys in Data and AI, and in looking forward to an AI-first enterprise. For software builders, I’d like to call out one of my favorite topics we touched upon and expand on it: “How do we make the Build vs Buy tradeoff in the AI enabled world, where the cost of building software is rapidly approaching zero?” I call the answer “You still own the pager!” It's a seductive idea. What used to take weeks and months now takes hours and days with AI coding tools. There are endless tweets and blogs about how software is free to produce, an idea that has recently wiped billions from the market cap of SaaS companies. AI has changed the world of software engineering and that trend will only accelerate. But here's what hasn't changed: 𝐀𝐈 𝐜𝐚𝐧 𝐰𝐫𝐢𝐭𝐞 𝐚𝐥𝐥 𝐭𝐡𝐞 𝐜𝐨𝐝𝐞. 𝐘𝐨𝐮 𝐬𝐭𝐢𝐥𝐥 𝐨𝐰𝐧 𝐭𝐡𝐞 𝐩𝐚𝐠𝐞𝐫. The cost of writing the initial code might be near zero. But when you run that code in production, you have to think of the code as both an asset and a potential liability. You have to maintain it, secure it, debug it at 3am, understand every edge case, and keep it reliable as your scale changes. For most companies, the right call is the same as it's always been — buy or leverage for anything that isn't core to your differentiation. Use whatever mature solution fits and works for you. Save the custom builds for the systems that are genuinely unique to your product. Leverage AI to double down on building the real differentiators for your product. We also discussed what this transformation means for software engineering teams - the need for reinvention at many levels - leadership to engineers to product managers. When implementation cost becomes near zero, the bottleneck shifts. The important skill isn’t how to build something, but deciding what to build in the first place, and iterate super quickly without sacrificing quality, reliability and scale. That judgement, taste and customer understanding becomes the new premium. Thrilled to share a warm and free-flowing conversation “AI will not save you, Reinvention Will!” with my respected friend [Kapil Surlaker](https://www.linkedin.com/in/kapilsurlaker?trk=public_post_reshare-text) on [Gopi Rangan](https://www.linkedin.com/in/gopirangan?trk=public_post_reshare-text)'s Sure Shot Entrepreneur series. Podcast: [https://lnkd.in/g5Vveksc](https://lnkd.in/g5Vveksc?trk=public_post_reshare-text) Youtube: [https://lnkd.in/gEmtiiZY](https://lnkd.in/gEmtiiZY?trk=public_post_reshare-text) Kapil and I have been fellow travelers in Silicon Valley, starting our careers building in Oracle’s RDBMS kernel, and later helping build the tech + teams behind LinkedIn’s Data and AI infrastructure. At LinkedIn, my journey lasted one infra generation, Kapil’s lasted three. The podcast is nerdy, shows a confirmation bias around infra in topics and shares names from our trip down memory lane. Here are a few teaser insights (e,g, “Data as Success” is a new phrase we coined and hope to trademark but didn't spend enough time on!) to get you started. (1) When the Google OG Jeff Dean offers advice re Databases and Systems, take it! Insider hint: SIGMOD/SOCC Indianapolis 2010 + LinkedIn Espresso! (2) Data before AI! - AI happens only if you invest in your Data Foundations and Operations first. LinkedIn had been running AI workloads for over a decade; it worked due to heavy lifting and hardening of data foundations, governance and operations over many years. When the GenAI moment arrived, LinkedIn wasn’t starting from zero. (3) You still own the code and the pager! – Choose carefully what you want to build vs buy in the AI first SDLC world. The cost of building and generating code with AI is only a small part of making the new enterprise work. (4) External AI Model supermarkets need thoughtful design! - Research lab LLM’s are here to stay and they are perhaps the new “Data as a Service”. But, there is no one model to rule them all. Model flexibility avoids lock-in and helps optimize for use cases if enabled with right orchestration infrastructure. Prepare for this new reference architecture! This is not trivial. (5) Reinvent or Go Extinct! – Tech and Product leadership in enterprises have to reinvent themselves rapidly in this AI first world, or you go extinct! In a world where the code generation cost is rapidly going to zero, you now have to optimize for rapid prototyping rather than writing detailed product specs and iterating quickly based on customer feedback. This also means quicker obsolescence of code, so you have to plan for much shorter cycles than before. (6) Talent matters! Cultivating engineering Culture and Talent should be viewed as an art, not just a science. Great leaders learn from their past e.g. Both BG and Kapil learnt from their formative years at the Oracle’s world-class RDBMS engineering culture.
